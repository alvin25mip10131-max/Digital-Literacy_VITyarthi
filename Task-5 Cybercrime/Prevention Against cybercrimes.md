# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students in India

Cybercrime is on the rise, especially among young adults who are active on digital platforms. Follow this checklist to protect yourself from online threats.

---

## General Prevention Tips

### 1. Use Strong, Unique Passwords
Make your passwords complex by mixing lowercase letters, numbers and symbols. Never use the password on multiple sites. A password manager can help you store them safely.

* Always use a password for each account.

* A password manager is a way to keep track of them.

### 2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Turn on two-factor authentication on accounts like email, social media and banking apps. This keeps you safe even if someone knows your password.

* It adds a layer of protection.

* Use it on all accounts that have information.

### 3. Be Wary of Unsolicited Messages
Don't click on links or download files from people you don't know on WhatsApp, Telegram, SMS or email. Scammers often pretend to be friends or companies.

* Be cautious of messages that ask for info.

* Check who the sender is before taking any action.

### 4. Verify Before Sharing Personal Information
Don't share your Aadhaar number, PAN or passport details with websites or people you don't trust. Scammers use tricks to get your data.

* Check if a website is secure before entering info.

* Don't share info with strangers.

### 5. Keep Devices and Apps Updated
Regularly update your phone, laptop and apps. Updates often fix security problems that hackers can use.

* Regular updates keep your devices safe.

* Check for updates often to stay protected.

### 6. Review App Permissions
Check what permissions you've given to apps on your phone. Don't give apps access to things they don't need like contacts or location.

* Review permissions regularly.

* Remove access, for apps that don't need it.

---

## UPI / Financial Safety Tips

### 7. Never Share UPI PIN, OTP, or Card Details
No bank representative, customer support agent, or government official will ever ask for your UPI PIN, OTP, or debit/credit card details. Treat any such request as a scam immediately.

### 8. Use UPI Apps with Caution
- Always check the payee name before confirming the payment
- Enable transaction alerts via SMS and email
- Set daily transaction limits on your bank account
- Never scan QR codes from unknown or untrusted sources

## Reporting Mechanism

If you become a victim of cybercrime or notice suspicious activity:

 **Method**
 
  **National Cyber Crime Portal**
  **Cyber Helpline Number**
  
 **Details** 

 [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) 
 **1930** (24x7 toll-free) 

Immediately report any financial fraud to **1930** to block fraudulent transactions and initiate the complaint process.
